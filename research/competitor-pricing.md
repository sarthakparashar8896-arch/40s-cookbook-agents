## Key Observations

### Pricing Tiers
- **Budget (₹19–₹25/100g):** Patanjali and Mother's Recipe in 500g–1kg formats. Patanjali's official-site prices are cheapest, but third-party marketplace prices can be 10–20% higher.
- **Mainstream (₹30–₹55/100g):** Priya, Mother's Recipe in smaller packs. This is the dominant grocery shelf tier.
- **Premium (₹90–₹120/100g):** FarmDidi and Naagin. 3–5× the mainstream price/gram. Both justify it through artisan/clean-label positioning.

### Platform Distribution Gaps
- **FarmDidi** is absent from Blinkit and Zepto — a significant distribution gap given quick-commerce's dominance in urban premium grocery.
- **Naagin** is not confirmed on Blinkit/Zepto (only on Swiggy Instamart and Flipkart/Amazon). Quick-commerce listing would meaningfully expand impulse reach.
- **Patanjali** has weak quick-commerce presence (only Blinkit, no Zepto confirmed), relying on its own store network and Amazon/Flipkart.

### Promotional Depth
- **Mother's Recipe** runs the deepest discounts on quick-commerce: 1kg packs at 26% off MRP on Blinkit — effectively using larger SKUs as basket-building tools.
- **Priya** prices cluster tightly at ₹103 for 300g across Blinkit, Zepto, and Flipkart — suggesting tight MAP control.
- **FarmDidi** prices on Amazon.in match the D2C site exactly (₹299 for 325g) — no channel conflict.

### Variant Breadth
- **Mother's Recipe** has the widest variant portfolio: Punjabi, Andhra Avakaya, Bengali, Kashmiri, Mixed, Lime, Green Chilli, Garlic, Choondo and more.
- **FarmDidi** has the deepest mango assortment: Homemade, Punjabi, Sweet, Chunda, Avakaya, Dry — 6 mango SKUs alone.
- **Patanjali** has core variants only (mango, lemon, mixed, garlic, amla, ginger) with no regional specialisation.

### Premium White Space
- There is a clear **price gap between ₹55 and ₹90/100g** — no brand sits in the "accessible premium" zone.
- A new brand entering at **₹60–₹75/100g** (e.g., 300g pack at ₹180–₹225) could own the mid-premium tier currently unoccupied.
